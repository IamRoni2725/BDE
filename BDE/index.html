BDE Process: An Inside Look

Full information on what needs to be done inside each step.

1 Receiving Leads

Receive a list of leads from the internal team on a daily or weekly basis. In this list, you will find these details:

  • Company Name, Contact Person: To reach the right person.
  • Email, Phone: For direct communication.
  • Business Size: To understand the client's scale and what their needs are.

How to Organize:

  • Excel Sheet/Google Sheet: If the number of leads is low, you can track them with these. Be sure to add columns for Status (New, Contacted, Pitch) and Notes.
  • CRM (Customer Relationship Management): Such as HubSpot or Salesforce. If there are a large number of leads, create a record for each lead in the CRM so everything remains organized.
2 Initial Contact / Outreach

Making first contact with the lead. The goal here is only to generate interest, not to sell the entire product.

Tips for the job:

  • Personalized Message: Do some research on the lead's company or their role. Send them a personalized message, like: "Hi [Name], I saw your company [Company Name]'s recent project. We are experts in [problem], and we might be able to help you..."
  • Channels: Use email, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or calls. In the beginning, contact them through just one channel.
  • Value Proposition: Don't just tell them what your product is. Explain which of their problems it will solve.
3 Product/Service Pitch

When a client is interested, give them detailed information. This should include:

  • Features & Benefits: Explain each feature by connecting it to a benefit. For example, "This feature makes your work faster, which will save you time."
  • Pricing: Explain the pricing models in a clear and transparent manner. There should be no hidden charges.
  • Case Studies/Previous Work: Show previous successful projects or case studies. This increases the client's trust.

Important:

Always focus on the client's problems and needs. Answer their questions, rather than just selling your product.

4 Follow-up

Consistent follow-up is necessary to close a deal. Pay attention to timing and consistency.

Follow-up Tips:

  • Timing: Don't disturb the client. Follow up after one or two days, and be sure to contact them once a week.
  • Value Addition: Add some new value with each follow-up, such as: "Here is a new case study for you," or "We have added a new feature that might be helpful to you."
  • Reminders: Set reminders in your calendar so no lead is missed.
5 Deal Closing

When the client is ready to make a final decision, work on negotiation and deal finalization.

Tasks:

  • Negotiate: Deal on pricing or terms.
  • Contract: Forward the contract and billing details to the internal team.
  • Finalization: Make all the next steps clear to the client, such as the onboarding process.
6 Reporting

Create a report to track your work and keep the team updated.

The report should contain these details:

  • Contacts Made: How many new leads were contacted.
  • Pitched Clients: How many clients were pitched.
  • Follow-ups: How many follow-up calls/emails were made.
  • Deals Closed/Pending: How many deals were closed and how many are in process.
